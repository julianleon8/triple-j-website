# Pricing & Positioning — Triple J Metal LLC
*Last updated: 2026-04-15 via NotebookLM*

## Market Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Product | Price Range | Provider Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Basic 20x20 bolted carport | $1,795 | Digital broker (Alan's Factory Outlet) |
| 2 | Heavy-duty bolted kit 20x20 | $2,200–$3,500 | Regional dealers |
| 3 | Custom welded 20x20 carport | **$4,500–$7,500** | Triple J (permanent structure) |
| 4 | Commercial red iron I-beam 20x20 | $8,000–$12,000 | Commercial builders |

## Triple J's Price Justification
Triple J sits in Tier 3 because their structures are:
- **Custom welded on-site** — not a factory kit
- **Permanent real estate improvements** (adds appraised value)
- **Turnkey** — concrete and structure in one contract; no sub-coordination cost

The key framing: don't compare to bolted kits — compare to **permanent construction**.

## Objection Handling: "It's more expensive than Alan's"
- Alan's ships a kit — you do site prep, permits, and find a concrete contractor separately
- Real all-in cost of an Alan's kit with site prep and concrete = $4,000–$6,000 anyway
- Triple J's structure: permanent, welded, warranted, and built by the same crew who pours your slab

## Premium Anchors for Upsell
- 12-gauge frame upgrade (vs standard 14-gauge): stronger, quieter in wind, required for 140 MPH rating
- WeatherXL™ color coat on panels: UV resistance, 30-year color warranty
- Drip Stop anti-condensation felt on underside of roofing
- Concrete pad upgrade: 4,000 PSI (vs standard 3,000 PSI) for Blackland Prairie expansion clay

## Quote Strategy Notes
- Start with structure size → frame gauge → panel type → color → concrete → timeline
- Never show "starting at" pricing — always quote based on actual spec
- Military/first-responder discount: mention as a step in the quote form or post-quote follow-up
