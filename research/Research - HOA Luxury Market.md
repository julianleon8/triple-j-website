## Why Standard Metal Sheds Fail HOAs
- HOA architectural guidelines prohibit utility-grade appearance
- Visible fasteners (PBR panels with exposed screws) look industrial
- Color must match or complement the home exterior
- Structure must appear "integrated" with the property aesthetic

## What Triple J Should Offer This Market
These buyers need premium panel options. MetalMax product hierarchy:
- **PBR panels** — standard, exposed fasteners, fine for residential/agricultural
- **Board & Batten panels** — farmhouse aesthetic, works for craftsman/modern homes
- **MaxLoc / MaxSeam / MaxSnap** — concealed-fastener standing seam panels, highest end, HOA-compliant

**Selling points:**
- Single-contract turnkey: wealthy buyers do NOT want to coordinate a concrete sub + metal erector separately
- Custom color matching to home palette
- Stamped engineering drawings available if HOA requires permit submission
- "Built to HOA standards, not just built"

## Messaging Framework
- Lead with the aesthetic angle, not the utility angle
- Emphasize concealed-fastener options and color choices
- Reference HOA by name when possible (Heritage Oaks, Bella Charca) — these neighborhoods are status markers for residents
- Price positioning: these buyers paid $500K+ for their home; a $10K–$15K custom carport is proportionate
