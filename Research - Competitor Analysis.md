# Competitor Analysis — Triple J Metal LLC
*Last updated: 2026-04-15 via NotebookLM*

## Mueller Inc.
- **Profile:** 90-year institutional benchmark, 36 branch locations, vertically integrated manufacturing
- **Tech:** HOVER® 3D smartphone modeling to reduce buyer hesitation
- **Financing:** Up to 84 months
- **Weakness:** Rigid sales policy — customers must offload their own materials and collect custom orders within 10 days to avoid fees. No turnkey concrete.

## Safeguard Metal Buildings
- **Profile:** Direct regional challenger, Troy TX and Waco TX
- **Strength:** Heavy warranty marketing — limited lifetime vertical roof warranty, 20-year rust-through guarantee on heavy-duty frames
- **Critical weakness:** Warranties are explicitly **voided** if installation site is not level within 24 inches OR if frame touches K-9 urine or wet timber. They **do not pour concrete** — leaving a massive service gap.

## Alan's Factory Outlet
- **Profile:** National digital broker model, dominates with extreme price transparency
- **Price anchor:** Basic 20x20 bolted carport for $1,795.00
- **Weakness:** Requires 10–17% deposit, forces customer to handle own site prep and permits. No local presence. No welding.

## East Texas Carports
- **Profile:** Regional kit dealer
- **Weaknesses:**
  - 10% non-refundable deposit
  - Hidden fees: $75 for 45-degree angle cuts
  - Explicitly excludes site preparation
  - Lead times: 4–12 weeks, sometimes up to **16 weeks**
- **Opportunity:** "Same-week" framing destroys this competitor on timeline

## Texas Metal Buildings (CTX)
- **Profile:** "Built-on-site" model, 12-gauge and 26-gauge upgrades
- **Claims:** 130 MPH wind rating, no third-party contractors
- **Note:** Directly competes with Triple J on the welded/permanent angle — monitor closely

## Peak Steel Buildings
- **Profile:** 100% commercial space, engineering red iron I-beam buildings
- **Lead times:** 6–8 weeks
- **Not a direct competitor** — plays in commercial, not residential/carport

## Leland's Metal Buildings
- **Profile:** High-volume manufacturer, 500+ building types, Rent-to-Own via AFG Rentals
- **Note:** Name causes confusion — "L&E" refers to Leland's, L&E Construction (luxury home builders), and L&E Development (remodelers). Opportunity to differentiate on local identity.

## "Central Texas Builders" Name Confusion
- "Central Texas Builders" returns luxury home construction firms, not metal building providers
- Explicitly targeting Central Texas metal buildings creates positioning clarity competitors lack

## Triple J's Differentiation vs All Above
- Turnkey: concrete + structure in **one contract** (nobody else does this)
- Welded red iron on-site (not bolted kits shipped to customer)
- Same-week scheduling (vs 4–16 week wait lists)
- Local Temple TX family business (vs national brands or regional dealers)
